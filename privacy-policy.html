Plain English Summary: Tap Legal records audio and location during emergencies, stores it securely, and shares it only with the legal professionals you choose. We do not sell your data. Ever.

1. Who We Are

Tap Legal ("we," "us," or "our") is a legal-technology application developed and operated from Monterey, California. Our contact email is taplegalapp1@mail.com. Tap Legal provides instant legal presence during police encounters through one-tap activation, automatic recording, and secure evidence storage.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide emergency legal presence services and connect you with licensed attorneys
  • To preserve and secure audio and incident evidence on your behalf
  • To compute escalation scores that determine the urgency of your situation
  • To send your incident data to legal professionals you have authorized
  • To maintain a full, tamper-evident audit trail of all emergency activations
  • To improve app reliability, performance, and user experience
  • To comply with applicable laws and legal obligations

4. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party for commercial purposes.

We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Legal professionals you authorize: When you activate an emergency, your incident data and recordings may be shared with attorneys or legal contacts you have designated in the app.
  • Service providers: We use Supabase (database and storage infrastructure) to store your data securely. Supabase processes data on our behalf and is contractually prohibited from using your data for any other purpose.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required by a valid court order, subpoena, or applicable law. We will notify you of such requests to the extent permitted by law.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred. We will notify you before your data becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

5. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide services. Incident recordings and evidence are retained for a minimum of 7 years to support potential legal proceedings, unless you request earlier deletion and no active legal matter requires retention. Account data is deleted within 30 days of account closure.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • AES-256 encryption for all stored recordings and evidence files
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all data in transit
  • Row-Level Security (RLS) in our database — you can only access your own data
  • SHA-256 integrity hashing for all uploaded evidence files
  • Pseudonymized user identifiers in all analytics and embeddings
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. We will notify you of any breach affecting your data within 72 hours of discovery, as required by applicable law.

7. Your Rights (California Residents — CCPA)

As a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., legal hold).
  • Right to Correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out: We do not sell personal information, so this right is not applicable.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights.

To exercise these rights, contact us at taplegalapp1@mail.com. We will respond within 45 days.

8. Children's Privacy

Tap Legal is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately and we will delete it.

9. Audio Recording and California Law

California Penal Code § 632 generally requires all-party consent for recording confidential communications. However, recording police officers performing their official duties in public spaces is protected under the First Amendment. Tap Legal is designed for use in public encounters with law enforcement. You are responsible for understanding and complying with applicable recording laws in your jurisdiction. Tap Legal does not provide legal advice.
